## Part 1: Minnesota Cannabis Regulatory Timeline

### May 2023: HF 100 Signed — Adult-Use Legalization
Governor Walz signed HF 100 into law on May 30, 2023, legalizing adult-use cannabis in Minnesota. The Office of Cannabis Management (OCMM) was created as the new regulatory authority.

### 2023–2024: OCMM Build-Out
OCMM was built from scratch. Staff hired, rules drafted, licensing framework developed. Home cultivation (8 plants per household) became legal effective August 1, 2023. Retail licensing framework developed through 2023–2024.

### 2025: Retail Licensing Launch
Minnesota's first adult-use retail licenses issued in 2025. The retail market is in its earliest stage in 2026 — most operators are in the first year of licensed retail operations.

### 2026: Market Launch Phase
Minnesota is actively in launch mode. OCMM is enforcing but with an acknowledged new-program learning curve. The operators who build correct compliance infrastructure now are ahead of the enforcement maturation curve.

## Part 2: Key Compliance Obligations for Launch-Phase Operators

### OCMM vs. Other Green States — What's Different
Minnesota's program has several features that distinguish it from other green states:

| Feature | Minnesota | Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Home cultivation | 8 plants per household (most permissive in green states) | CO: 6, OH: 6, MI: 12, MO: 6 |
| Social equity priority | Strong equity licensing provisions | Similar to NY/MO |
| Tracking system | OCMM-designated system | Confirm current system with OCMM |
| Security retention | 90 days | Same as CO, OH, MI, MO, PA, NY |
| MDA pesticide authority | Two-authority structure | Same as all green states |
