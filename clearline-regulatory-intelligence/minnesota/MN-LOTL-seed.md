## Minnesota Regulatory Framework Overview

**Governing Authority:** Office of Cannabis Management (OCM), created under Minn. Stat. § 342.01 et seq.

Minnesota's adult-use cannabis law (HF 100/SF 73, enacted 2023) established a regulatory framework with the following distinctive characteristics:

- **Equity-first licensing structure**: Lower-barrier licenses for social equity applicants, including individuals with prior cannabis convictions, communities disproportionately impacted by cannabis prohibition, and small operators
- **Local control preservation**: Cities and counties can opt out of allowing cannabis retail; they cannot impose stricter operational requirements than state law
- **Medical cannabis program integration**: Minnesota's legacy medical program (under the former MN Department of Health) is transitioning to OCM oversight
- **Staged rollout**: OCM has been issuing license types in stages; not all license categories were operational as of Q1 2026

### 1.2 Equity License Tiers

Minnesota created two equity license categories with distinct requirements:

**Social Equity Applicant** (Minn. Stat. § 342.17):
- Prior cannabis conviction (or family member with prior conviction)
- Resident of a census tract disproportionately affected by the war on drugs
- Veteran with eligible cannabis-related status
- Income below state threshold

**Microbusiness License** (Minn. Stat. § 342.40):
- Vertically integrated; limited to small-scale operations
- Plant count and product volume caps apply
- Lower fee structure; streamlined application

### 1.3 Core Licensing Obligations

- Annual renewal with OCM
- Background check required for all principal officers and owners
- Local authorization documentation required at application — municipal opt-out status must be verified
- Ownership disclosure — all beneficial owners ≥ 5%
- Financial source documentation
- Insurance requirements per OCM rules

## Section 2: Seed-to-Sale Tracking

### 2.1 Minnesota's Tracking System

OCM is implementing a state-managed seed-to-sale tracking system. As of April 2026, the system is in deployment stages — not all license categories have been fully onboarded. OCM has issued interim guidance on manual tracking for operators not yet integrated into the state system.

**Core tracking obligations (operative and forthcoming):**
- Plant-level tagging at propagation
- Harvest batch creation at harvest
- Transfer documentation before product movement
- Inventory reconciliation per OCM schedule
- Testing hold procedures — product cannot be sold pending test results

### 2.2 Tracking System Transition Note

Minnesota's tracking system transition from legacy medical program to OCM infrastructure is ongoing. Operators who were active in Minnesota's medical program face a dual-system period — maintaining legacy track-and-trace obligations while onboarding to the new OCM system. The transition timeline and any overlap obligations are documented in OCM bulletins (tracked separately).

## Section 3: Security Requirements

OCM security rules require:
- Camera coverage of all areas where cannabis is present
- Defined retention period (OCM rules; specific retention period to be confirmed against current OCM rule text)
- Visitor log requirements
- Alarm system with monitoring
- Badge system for employee access

**MN-Specific Note**: Minnesota's security rules are structured around the state's broader regulatory framework and have been issued in stages. Operators should verify current OCM rule text directly for the most recent security specifications — this seed volume will be updated as OCM finalizes its full security rule chapter.

## Section 4: Social Equity & Local Control

### 4.1 Social Equity Obligations

Minnesota's equity framework imposes ongoing obligations, not just at the licensing stage:
- Reporting requirements on equity program activities (Minn. Stat. § 342.17(d))
- Recapture provisions if equity commitments are not maintained
- OCM oversight of equity license transfers — equity conditions may not transfer with the license

### 4.2 Local Control Framework

Under Minn. Stat. § 342.13:
- Municipalities can prohibit cannabis retail operations by local ordinance
- Cannot impose stricter operational requirements than state law
- Must act by a defined deadline to prohibit; inaction = default opt-in
- Local prohibition can be reversed by ordinance or referendum

**Operator action**: Verify your municipality's opt-in/opt-out status before any investment. Municipal opt-out status is a license-level threshold — a state license does not override a municipal prohibition.

---

## Section 5: Testing Requirements

- All cannabis products must be tested by a licensed OCM testing facility before sale
- Required panels include potency, microbials, pesticide residues, and other OCM-specified tests
- Products in testing hold may not be sold, transferred, or destroyed without OCM authorization
- Testing facilities must meet OCM licensing requirements (accreditation framework in development as of April 2026)
