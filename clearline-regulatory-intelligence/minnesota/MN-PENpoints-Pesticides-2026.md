## Minnesota Pesticide Compliance Framework

**Two-authority structure:**
1. **OCMM (Minn. Stat. § 342 rules)**: Cannabis-specific pesticide requirements — permitted products, application log, re-entry intervals, IPM plan
2. **MDA (Minn. Stat. § 18B, Pesticide Control Law)**: Commercial pesticide applicator licensing — applies to cannabis cultivation facilities as agricultural operations

---

## MDA Applicator License Requirement

Minnesota's Pesticide Control Law requires commercial pesticide applications at a business operation to be made by or under the direct supervision of an MDA-licensed commercial pesticide applicator. Cannabis cultivation facilities are covered.

**Verification requirements:**
- In-house cultivation staff applying pesticides: MDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator license
- IPM contractors: MDA Pesticide Dealer License + appropriate applicator credentials
- Annual renewal; verify current status before every application

## Minnesota-Specific: New Program, Enforcement Posture Building

Minnesota's OCMM is one of the youngest cannabis regulatory agencies in the active states. The pesticide enforcement posture is still being established. Build the correct compliance infrastructure now — the enforcement teeth come after the market matures.

**Pre-harvest testing** is the same best practice in Minnesota as everywhere else: the only early-warning system available before a post-harvest positive test triggers product quarantine.

---

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — Minnesota

- [ ] All pesticides on premises EPA-registered and on OCMM/MDA permitted list
- [ ] Application log current and complete with MDA license numbers
- [ ] All commercial applicators hold current MDA license
- [ ] IPM contractors: MDA Pesticide Dealer License + applicator credentials verified
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented and enforced
- [ ] IPM plan on file with OCMM, current version
- [ ] Supplier pesticide disclosure obtained for all clone/plant purchases
- [ ] Pre-harvest pesticide residue testing in protocol
