## How to Use This Matrix

This matrix is designed for facility posting, training binder inclusion, and inspector-ready reference. It captures the operative pesticide compliance landscape for Colorado cannabis cultivation facilities as of April 2026.

- ✅ **Green**: Generally permitted with application log  
- ⚠️ **Yellow**: Conditional — verify label, rate, or application method before use  
- 🚫 **Red**: Prohibited — do not purchase, store, or use  
- 🔴 **Critical**: Prohibited with escalated enforcement — first offense triggers product quarantine

## Section C: Conditional Compounds — Verify Before Use

| Compound | Concern | Required Verification | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sulfur-based fungicides (wettable sulfur) | Temp/humidity burn risk; MED testing interaction | Check label for cannabis or food-crop use; temperature restrictions | Medium |
| Potassium bicarbonate | EPA registration status variable by product | Verify specific product has food-crop label; confirm EPA reg. # | Low–Medium |
| Essential oil blends (clove, rosemary, etc.) | Variable EPA registration across blend products | Verify per-product EPA registration number | Medium |
| Copper sulfate / copper octanoate | Rate-restricted; runoff concerns; residue accumulation | Verify label; log rate and frequency; CDPHE check | Medium |
| Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) | Not EPA-registered as a pesticide; may not meet Rule 3-1005(A) | Confirm EPA registration before use as pesticide | Medium |
| UV-C lighting (non-chemical) | Not a pesticide; no log required as compound | No application log needed; document as IPM tool | Low |

## Section D: Application Log — Required Fields

Every pesticide application at your Colorado cannabis facility must be logged with the following fields per Rule 3-1005(B):

| Field | Required | Example Entry |
|---|---|---|
| Product name (full) | ✅ | "Pyganic EC 1.4" |
| EPA Registration Number | ✅ | "EPA Reg. No. 1021-1771-67690" |
| Date of application | ✅ | "April 14, 2026" |
| Time of application | ✅ | "08:30 AM" |
| Applicator name (individual) | ✅ | "Maria G., IPM Lead" |
| Application rate | ✅ | "2 fl oz per gallon, 1 gallon per 500 sq ft" |
| Application method | ✅ | "Backpack sprayer, foliar" |
| Target pest/disease | ✅ | "Thrips (Frankliniella occidentalis)" |
| Treatment zone/area | ✅ | "Canopy Room C, rows 1–8" |
| Re-entry interval noted | ✅ | "48 hours — access restricted until 4/16/2026 08:30 AM" |

## Section E: Supply Chain Documentation Requirements

Per the evolving enforcement posture (2025 Enforcement Report; CDPHE March 2026 memo), operators must document pesticide inputs at every entry point in the supply chain:

| Supply Chain Point | Documentation Required | When to Obtain |
|---|---|---|
| Clone / plant purchase from supplier | Supplier pesticide disclosure (products used, last application date) | At point of purchase / before plant acceptance |
| Third-party IPM contractor application | Contractor's application log (same fields as Section D) | Day of application |
| Pre-packaged plant inputs (soils, media) | SDS (Safety Data Sheet) + label review for any embedded compounds | Before procurement |
| Wholesale flower / product acceptance | Pre-acceptance COA showing pesticide residue panel | Before receiving product |

## Section F: Response Protocol — If a Prohibited Compound Is Found

If a pesticide test result shows a prohibited compound:

1. **Do not sell, transfer, or destroy any affected product** — METRC hold required  
2. **Notify MED within 24 hours** — failure to self-report compounds the violation  
3. **Preserve the application log** — your log (or the absence of a log entry) will be central to the investigation  
4. **Identify the source** — supplier disclosure records and contractor logs are your primary defense  
5. **Engage qualified legal counsel** — the quarantine period's outcome can often be influenced by the quality of your response  
6. **Remediation testing** — standard not currently defined; follow MED's specific instructions
