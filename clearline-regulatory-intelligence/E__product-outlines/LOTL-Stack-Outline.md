# LOTL Stack — Product Outline

*ClearLine Regulatory Intelligence | Grey Matter Praxis*

---

## What It Is

The LOTL Stack is the statutory foundation. It compiles official statutes, regulation numbers, settled legal frameworks, and their direct operational implications into a single, citation-backed document. The law everyone knows — documented with the precision operators actually need.

This is not a summary. It is a structured, traceable compilation of every black-letter obligation in a given state's cannabis regulatory framework, organized by workflow and output-ready for SOPs, training materials, and evidence binders.

---

## Who It's For

- **Compliance managers** building or auditing internal policy frameworks
- **Multi-state operators** who need apples-to-apples citation structures across jurisdictions
- **Legal counsel** who need a pre-organized foundation before applying interpretive analysis
- **Investors and executives** conducting due diligence on state-specific regulatory exposure
- **Training leads** who need pinpoint citations attached to staff-facing obligations

---

## Document Architecture

Each LOTL Stack is organized by operational workflow. The structure follows a consistent schema across all 39 covered states, allowing for direct cross-state comparison.

### Core Sections

**1. Licensing Obligations**
Establishment licensing statutes, required document checklists, application procedures, and renewal timelines — with pinpoint citation to applicable code sections.
> *Colorado example: CRS 44-10-301(2)(a)-(f)*

**2. Seed-to-Sale Tracking**
Tracking system participation requirements, device and access controls, reconciliation standards, and data integrity obligations.
> *Colorado example: MED Rule 3-105(B)(1)-(4)*

**3. Security & Access Control**
Premises security standards, camera coverage requirements, retention schedules, and visitor control protocols.
> *Colorado example: MED Rule 3-225(A)-(C)*

**4. Packaging & Labeling**
THC symbol requirements, warning statement mandates, child-resistant standards, and label content obligations.
> *Colorado example: CRS 44-10-203 & MED Rule 3-1010*

**5. Waste Handling**
Rendering standards (unusable and unrecognizable), manifest requirements, and transport obligations.
> *Colorado example: MED Rule 3-1505*

**Additional sections** vary by state and may include: manufacturing and extraction standards, transportation and delivery, employee licensing, laboratory testing, and municipal/local overlay obligations.

---

## Output Format

- **SOP-ready text**: Language formatted for direct insertion into standard operating procedures without reformatting
- **Clause-level checklists**: Each obligation broken into a discrete, checkable item with pinpoint citation attached
- **Pinpoint citations**: Every item traceable to a specific statute or rule section, subsection, and paragraph
- **Change log**: Version-tracked against effective dates so operators know what changed and when
- **Evidence binder attachment**: Formatted for use in audit documentation without additional conversion

## Coverage

LOTL Stacks are available for all **39 adult-use and medical-regulated cannabis states** in the ClearLine coverage matrix. Each state receives its own standalone stack structured to that jurisdiction's specific statutory and regulatory framework.

Multi-state bundles (Regional Playbooks) and all-access packages are also available for operators covering multiple jurisdictions.

## How It Combines with Other Products

The LOTL Stack is the baseline layer. It documents what is required — the non-negotiable obligations.

It is designed to pair with:

- **Penumbrant Prose Manual** — layered over the LOTL Stack, the Penumbrant analysis identifies where those statutory obligations become ambiguous in practice, how regulators have exercised discretion, and where enforcement risk actually lives
- **Lil LOTL Stack** — a condensed version of the same statutory content, surfacing only the highest-priority obligations for fast-start teams or training contexts
- **Field Issues (Field Notes)** — ground-level documentation of where LOTL-compliant operators still experience friction, because the statute and operational reality don't always match
- **Margin Issues (Margin Notes)** — the political and policy climate surrounding the statutes, documenting forces that may move the LOTL baseline in the near term

> *"Baseline with LOTL to establish non-negotiable obligations. Layer over with Penumbrant to anticipate enforcement flex, ride out gray gaps, and see through cross-state patterns."*
> — ClearLine Framework
