## Executive Summary

Pennsylvania is the largest medical-only cannabis market in the United States. The OMM enforces a mature regulatory framework with DOH institutional discipline. The 2026 compliance priorities are operational: BioTrack fluency, pharmacist integration, 90-day security retention across multi-location portfolios, and — increasingly — strategic positioning for a potential adult-use transition.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 1: BioTrack THC — System Competency

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Operational  
**Trigger:** 28 Pa. Code § 1141.35, § 1141.57; OMM inspection pattern

BioTrack is not Metrc. Multi-state operators entering Pennsylvania with Metrc-trained staff are the highest-risk segment for BioTrack compliance failures. The errors are systematic, not isolated — they trace to structural misunderstanding of BioTrack's lot-based architecture.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Have all staff who touch tracking tasks completed BioTrack-specific training (not Metrc training)?
- Is BioTrack training documented as a separate competency in your training records?
- Has your BioTrack inventory been audited against physical counts specifically for structural BioTrack errors (not just discrepancies)?

**Recommended Posture:** BioTrack competency is a Pennsylvania-specific compliance investment. It cannot be assumed from Metrc experience. Dedicate training time and documentation specifically to BioTrack workflows.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 2: Pharmacist Operations Integration

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Structural  
**Trigger:** 28 Pa. Code § 1141.55; OMM inspection standard

Pennsylvania's pharmacist requirement is real and enforced. The gap between "pharmacist on the license application" and "pharmacist integrated into operations" is where OMM is finding violations.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Is your pharmacist actually integrated into dispensary operations — not just listed on the license?
- Can your pharmacist be reached for patient consultation during all dispensary operating hours?
- Is the pharmacist's involvement documented (consultation records, operational sign-offs)?

**Recommended Posture:** Document pharmacist involvement explicitly and operationally. If you use a telehealth consultation model, the consultation protocol, response times, and patient interaction records must be complete and accessible.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 3: 90-Day Retention Across Multi-Location Portfolio

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Infrastructure  
**Trigger:** 28 Pa. Code § 1141.25 / § 1141.53(i); aging medical-era install base

Pennsylvania's multi-location dispensary structure multiplies the 90-day retention infrastructure gap. A portfolio of 3–5 locations may have security systems installed across multiple years with inconsistent storage capacities and hardware ages.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Has every location in your dispensary organization portfolio been audited for 90-day retention compliance?
- Have you tested footage retrieval from Day 85 at every location?
- Are any of your systems more than 4 years old without hardware review?

**Recommended Posture:** Treat the security audit as a portfolio exercise, not a per-location event. A finding at one location with aging infrastructure suggests the entire portfolio needs review.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 4: Patient Purchase Limit Monitoring

**Risk Level:** 🟠 High — Operational  
**Trigger:** 28 Pa. Code § 1141.57(d); 35 P.S. § 10231.405

Pennsylvania's 30-day supply purchase limit — variable by physician recommendation — requires active monitoring at point of sale. Manual tracking is high-risk at volume.

**Recommended Posture:** POS systems integrated with BioTrack that track patient purchase totals against their physician's recommendation are the compliance standard. Manual systems are acceptable only for very low-volume operations with dedicated compliance staff.
