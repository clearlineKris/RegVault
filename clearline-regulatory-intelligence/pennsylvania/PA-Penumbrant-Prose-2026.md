## 1. BioTrack vs. Metrc — The Onboarding Risk

### The Gap
Pennsylvania uses BioTrack THC for seed-to-sale tracking. Most of the cannabis industry workforce has Metrc experience. BioTrack is structured differently — lot-based rather than individual package-based — and the workflow differences are non-trivial for staff trained on Metrc.

### The Drift
OMM inspections have flagged BioTrack compliance issues that trace to staff with Metrc muscle memory performing Pennsylvania tracking tasks incorrectly. The root cause is not malicious — it's a training gap.

### The Exposure
BioTrack errors in Pennsylvania create compliance findings that look the same as intentional tracking violations on paper. The enforcement record doesn't distinguish between "wrong system knowledge" and "intentional manipulation."

### The Posture
Any operator entering Pennsylvania from a Metrc state must implement BioTrack-specific training before any staff member touches tracking tasks. BioTrack training documentation should be part of every new hire onboarding in Pennsylvania operations.

## 2. Pharmacist Requirement — The Operations Layer

### The Gap
Pennsylvania requires dispensary organizations to have a pharmacist involved in operations (28 Pa. Code § 1141.55). This is a unique Pennsylvania requirement — no other green state has a pharmacist mandate. The rule doesn't specify exactly what "involved in operations" means in terms of hours, physical presence, or decision-making authority.

### The Drift
OMM has informally interpreted the pharmacist requirement as requiring on-site pharmacist availability during dispensary hours, or a telehealth pharmacist consultation structure. The specific compliance standard varies by how OMM interprets "available."

### The Exposure
Dispensary organizations that treat the pharmacist requirement as a checkbox — listing a pharmacist on their license application without integrating pharmacist involvement into operations — face OMM findings.

### The Posture
Document pharmacist involvement explicitly. If telehealth pharmacist consultation is your model, document the consultation protocol, response time commitments, and patient interaction records.

## 3. Multi-Location Dispensary Organization — The Coverage Compliance Risk

### The Gap
Pennsylvania's Dispensary Organization structure allows up to 3 locations (or 5 with OMM approval) under a single license. The license covers all locations — but each location has independent security, BioTrack, and compliance obligations.

### The Drift
OMM inspections treat each dispensary location as independently compliant or non-compliant, even though they operate under one organizational license. A finding at one location does not automatically affect others, but a pattern of findings across locations can trigger an organizational-level review.

### The Exposure
Multi-location dispensary organizations that allow compliance standards to vary across locations — typically because one location receives more management attention than others — face the risk that a finding at a lower-attention location triggers a portfolio-level OMM review.

### The Posture
Uniform compliance standards and inspection schedules across all dispensary locations. Treat each location as if it could be independently inspected at any time — because it can.

## 4. Patient Purchase Limits — The 30-Day Supply Variable

### The Gap
Pennsylvania's patient purchase limit is framed as a 30-day supply as recommended by the certifying practitioner — not a fixed gram or ounce limit. The certifying physician specifies the recommended dosing, and the dispensary is responsible for tracking the patient's purchases against that recommendation.

### The Drift
The 30-day supply model creates compliance ambiguity: what happens when a patient's physician changes their recommended dosing, or when a patient has multiple certifying physicians with different recommendations? BioTrack tracks purchases but does not automatically flag when a patient approaches or exceeds their practitioner's recommendation.

### The Exposure
Dispensaries that don't actively monitor patient purchase totals against their certifying physician's recommendation face patient service violations if a patient purchases beyond their 30-day supply.

### The Posture
POS systems that integrate with BioTrack and track patient purchase totals against their recommendation are the most defensible. Manual tracking of 30-day supply compliance is high-risk at volume.

---

## 5. Adult-Use Legalization Risk Horizon

### The Gap
Pennsylvania is medical-only in 2026, but adult-use legalization efforts have been active. The compliance risk for existing licensed operators: if and when adult-use passes, the transition will require a rapid operational rebuild similar to Missouri's 2023 experience.

### The Posture
Medical operators who build compliance infrastructure to standards that would also satisfy adult-use requirements (90-day retention, robust Metrc/BioTrack discipline, packaging QA) are better positioned for transition than operators who build only to the minimum medical standard.
