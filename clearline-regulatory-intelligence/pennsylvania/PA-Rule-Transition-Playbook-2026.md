## Part 1: Pennsylvania Cannabis Regulatory Timeline

### 2016: Medical Marijuana Act — Act 16
Pennsylvania enacted the MMA on April 17, 2016. One of the later East Coast medical programs, but one of the more regulated: DOH oversight, pharmacist requirement, restricted forms, BioTrack tracking.

### 2017–2018: Program Build-Out
First Grower/Processor and Dispensary Organization permits issued. Program went live with initial dispensary sales in February 2018.

### 2019–2022: Program Maturation
Patient registry growth. Dispensary organization expansions (3 to 5 locations). Grower/Processor facility upgrades. OMM's inspection and enforcement infrastructure matured.

### 2022: Forms and Product Expansion
OMM expanded permitted medical marijuana forms to include flower (dry leaf). Prior to 2022, only oils, tinctures, concentrates, and topicals were permitted — not whole flower. The flower expansion required Grower/Processors to add cultivation infrastructure and Dispensaries to update their dispensing protocols.

**Compliance impact:** The flower expansion created new compliance requirements — moisture testing, new packaging requirements for dry leaf, updated application log protocols for outdoor-adjacent cultivation.

### 2023–2026: Adult-Use Legalization Discussions
Pennsylvania's legislature has been debating adult-use legalization since 2021, with multiple bill introductions but no enactment as of April 2026. The compliance implication: operators are building programs in a medical framework while preparing for a potential transition.
