## Oklahoma Pesticide Compliance Framework

**Two-authority structure:**
1. **OMMA (OAC 310:681-5-5)**: Cannabis-specific pesticide rules — permitted products, application log, re-entry intervals, IPM plan
2. **ODAFF (Oklahoma Pesticide Act, 2 O.S. § 3-81 et seq.)**: Commercial pesticide applicator licensing — applies to cannabis facilities as agricultural operations

---

## ODAFF Applicator License Requirement

Oklahoma's Pesticide Act requires commercial pesticide applications at a business operation to be made by or under the supervision of an ODAFF-licensed commercial pesticide applicator. Cannabis cultivation facilities are covered.

**Verification requirements:**
- In-house IPM staff must hold ODAFF Commercial Pesticide Applicator license
- IPM contractors must hold ODAFF Pest Control Business License + appropriate applicator credentials
- Annual renewal required; verify current status before every contractor engagement

## Oklahoma-Specific: Acquisition Supply Chain Risk

In Oklahoma's consolidation environment, acquired cultivation facilities may have pesticide application logs with gaps, inconsistencies, or prohibited compound histories. All acquired facilities should undergo a pesticide application log audit and pre-operation pesticide residue testing before resuming production.

---

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — Oklahoma

- [ ] All pesticides on premises EPA-registered and on OMMA/ODAFF permitted list
- [ ] Application log current and complete with ODAFF license numbers
- [ ] All commercial applicators hold current ODAFF license
- [ ] IPM contractors: ODAFF Pest Control Business License + applicator verified
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented and enforced
- [ ] IPM plan on file with OMMA, current version
- [ ] Supplier pesticide disclosure obtained for all biomass/trim purchases
- [ ] Pre-harvest pesticide residue testing in protocol
- [ ] Acquired facility pesticide history audited before resuming production
