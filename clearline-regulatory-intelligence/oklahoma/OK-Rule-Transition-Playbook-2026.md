## Part 1: Oklahoma Cannabis Regulatory Timeline

### 2018: State Question 788 — Medical Program Launch
Oklahoma's initial medical marijuana framework — extremely permissive: no plant count limit, low fees, limited local authority. The licensing rush began immediately.

### 2019–2021: Peak Licensing
Oklahoma became the state with the most cannabis licenses per capita nationally — exceeding 9,000 licensed businesses. OMMA built its inspection infrastructure while the market was already operating at full scale.

### 2021: SB 1100 — Strengthened Enforcement and Anti-Diversion
Increased penalties for diversion; added requirements for security, Metrc compliance, and pesticide documentation. This was Oklahoma's "we built this too fast" correction — adding compliance requirements to a program that had intentionally minimized them at launch.

**Operator impact:** Operators who had operated under the permissive 2018 framework with minimal IPM, security, or Metrc discipline were required to upgrade their compliance infrastructure to SB 1100 standards. Many didn't.

### 2023: State Question 820 — Adult-Use Fails
Oklahoma voters rejected adult-use legalization in March 2023. The market, which had priced adult-use legalization into its growth projections, contracted further. This is the pivot point for understanding 2026 compliance dynamics — Oklahoma is a consolidating medical market indefinitely.

### 2023–2025: Consolidation Wave
Mass consolidation through distress acquisitions, nonrenewals, and OMMA enforcement actions against non-compliant operators. The surviving licensee base is more concentrated and under more intensive OMMA scrutiny.
