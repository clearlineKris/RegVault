## Oklahoma Security Framework

### OAC 310:681-2-10 — Premises Security
- Camera coverage: all entry/exit, all storage, all sales/dispensary, all cultivation, all loading/unloading
- **60-day minimum footage retention** (lower than Ohio/Michigan at 90 days)
- Visitor log with name, date, time, purpose, escort
- Security plan on file; updated within 30 days of material change

### OAC 310:681-2-11 — Alarm Systems
- 24/7 central monitoring by licensed monitoring company
- Panic buttons at POS and cash-handling locations
- Motion detection in cultivation and storage

---

## The Oklahoma Security Aging Problem

Oklahoma's security risk is not the retention standard — 60 days is achievable with modest storage. The risk is **aging infrastructure** from the 2019–2021 licensing boom and the financial pressure of market consolidation.

### What Fails in Aging Systems
- **Hard drives:** DVR/NVR hard drives have typical lifespans of 3–5 years under continuous write cycles. Oklahoma's 2020 installs are past or approaching end-of-life. Failure means no footage — the entire retention period is lost at once
- **Camera drift:** Cameras shift after mounting over years; the corner that was in frame in 2021 may be out of frame in 2026
- **Monitoring contract lapses:** Distressed operators on auto-pay who don't notice when a monitoring company changes ownership or ceases service
- **Expansion coverage gaps:** Unreported facility modifications leaving new cultivation rooms or storage areas uncovered

### The 30-Day Update Requirement
OAC 310:681-2-10 requires security plan updates within 30 days of any material change. "Material change" = new rooms, new entrances, new storage configurations, layout changes. Most operators who expanded their footprint in 2020–2023 did not file security plan updates within 30 days.

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — Oklahoma Security

- [ ] DVR/NVR hard drive age verified — systems >4 years old should have hard drives replaced
- [ ] 60-day footage retention capacity confirmed at current camera count + resolution
- [ ] Footage retrieval from Day 55 tested and confirmed
- [ ] Camera coverage audit against current facility footprint (including post-2021 modifications)
- [ ] Security plan updated within 30 days of any material change (OAC 310:681-2-10)
- [ ] Alarm monitoring contract: current, verified active, correct facility address on file
- [ ] Panic buttons at all POS + cash handling, tested
- [ ] Visitor log — digital, all fields required, accessible for immediate review
- [ ] Acquired facilities: independent security audit before resuming operation
