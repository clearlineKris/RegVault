## Oklahoma Regulatory Framework Overview

**Governing Authority:** Oklahoma Medical Marijuana Authority (OMMA), a division of the Oklahoma State Department of Health (OSDH).

Oklahoma operates a **medical-only** program. State Question 820 (adult-use) was voted down by Oklahomans in March 2023. All licensed activity in 2026 operates under the medical marijuana framework.

Oklahoma's program is unusual nationally for its permissive structure: no patient cap, no cultivator plant count limits (outside facility footprint), low license fees at launch. The result: the highest license density in the country at peak (2021–2022), followed by severe oversupply and market consolidation.

## Section 1: Licensing Obligations

### 1.1 Statutory Foundation
- **63 O.S. § 421**: Grower license
- **63 O.S. § 422**: Processor license
- **63 O.S. § 423**: Dispensary license
- **63 O.S. § 425**: Transportation license
- **63 O.S. § 426**: Testing laboratory license
- **63 O.S. § 427.14**: Commercial grower license (larger-scale cultivation)
- **OAC 310:681-1-1 et seq.**: Operational requirements for all license types

### 1.2 License Categories — Oklahoma 2026

| License Type | Statute | Key Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Grower | 63 O.S. § 421 | No state plant count limit; facility footprint governs |
| Processor | 63 O.S. § 422 | Extraction method disclosure to OMMA |
| Dispensary | 63 O.S. § 423 | Patient registry verification required for each sale |
| Transporter | 63 O.S. § 425 | Required for all inter-facility product movement |
| Testing Laboratory | 63 O.S. § 426 | ISO 17025 accreditation; independence requirements |
| Commercial Grower | 63 O.S. § 427.14 | Expanded-scale; higher fees |
| Research | 63 O.S. § 427.2 | Research and development operations |

### 1.3 Patient Registry and Verification
Oklahoma's medical system requires dispensaries to verify patient registry status at the point of sale. Every sale must be recorded in the patient registry system against a valid patient or caregiver card.

**Patient purchase limits (OAC 310:681-3-2):**
- Patients: 3 ounces of marijuana per single purchase; monthly purchase limits apply
- Caregivers: purchase on behalf of up to 5 patients

### 1.4 Compliance Checklist — Licensing

- [ ] Current OMMA license certificate posted at licensed premises (OAC 310:681-2-3)
- [ ] Ownership and principal disclosure current (OAC 310:681-2-5)
- [ ] Background checks current for all owners and key employees (63 O.S. § 421(C))
- [ ] Annual renewal filed within OMMA renewal window
- [ ] Patient registry verification process operational at dispensary (OAC 310:681-3-2)

## Section 2: Seed-to-Sale Tracking

### 2.1 Statutory Foundation
- **63 O.S. § 427.14**: Mandatory seed-to-sale tracking system
- **OAC 310:681-7**: Metrc tracking requirements
- Oklahoma uses **Metrc** as its statewide seed-to-sale tracking system

### 2.2 Metrc Obligations — 2026

| Obligation | Rule Ref | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Plant tag at propagation | OAC 310:681-7-2(a) | Day of propagation |
| Harvest batch creation | OAC 310:681-7-2(b) | Day of harvest |
| Transfer manifest (pre-departure) | OAC 310:681-7-3 | Before vehicle moves |
| Inventory reconciliation | OAC 310:681-7-2(e) | Weekly; documented |
| Discrepancy report | OAC 310:681-7-2(f) | Within 3 business days of discovery |

### 2.3 Oklahoma-Specific: Patient Sale Recording
Every dispensary sale must be recorded in both Metrc AND the patient registry. The two-system recording requirement is a source of compliance friction — mismatches between Metrc sales records and patient registry records are an OMMA inspection finding category.

### 2.4 Compliance Checklist — Tracking

- [ ] All plants tagged at propagation (OAC 310:681-7-2(a))
- [ ] Harvest batches created same-day (OAC 310:681-7-2(b))
- [ ] Transfer manifests before departure (OAC 310:681-7-3)
- [ ] Weekly Metrc reconciliation against physical count (OAC 310:681-7-2(e))
- [ ] Patient registry sales records match Metrc daily sales records (OAC 310:681-3-2)
- [ ] Discrepancy reports filed within 3 business days (OAC 310:681-7-2(f))

## Section 3: Security & Access Control

### 3.1 Rule Foundation
- **OAC 310:681-2-10**: Security requirements
  - Camera coverage: all entry/exit, all storage, all sales areas, all cultivation areas
  - Retention: **60-day** minimum (Oklahoma's requirement; lower than Ohio/Michigan's 90 days)
  - Visitor log and restricted access
- **OAC 310:681-2-11**: Alarm systems — 24/7 monitoring

### 3.2 Security Requirements Matrix

| Area | Camera Required | Retention | Restricted |
|---|---|---|---|
| All exterior entry/exit | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |
| Retail/dispensary floor | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |
| Vault/storage | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |
| Processing rooms | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |
| Cultivation canopy | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |
| Loading/unloading | ✅ | 60 days | ✅ |

### 3.3 Compliance Checklist — Security

- [ ] 60-day footage retention storage capacity verified (OAC 310:681-2-10)
- [ ] Camera coverage audit against current facility footprint
- [ ] Visitor log: name, date, time, purpose, escort employee
- [ ] 24/7 alarm monitoring contract current (OAC 310:681-2-11)
- [ ] Security plan updated within 30 days of material facility change

## Section 4: Pesticide Compliance

### 4.1 Rule Foundation
- **OAC 310:681-5-5**: Pesticide requirements for growers
  - EPA-registered pesticides only; labeled for cannabis, food crop, or non-phytotoxic
  - Application log required
  - Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food and Forestry (ODAFF) applicator licensing may apply
  - 48-hour re-entry interval
  - IPM plan required

### 4.2 Compliance Checklist — Pesticides

- [ ] All pesticides EPA-registered and on OMMA/ODAFF permitted list (OAC 310:681-5-5)
- [ ] Application log complete and specific
- [ ] ODAFF commercial applicator credential verified for all applications
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented
- [ ] IPM plan on file, current

## Section 5: Packaging & Labeling

### 5.1 Rule Foundation
- **OAC 310:681-4**: Product packaging standards
  - Child-resistant packaging
  - Oklahoma universal symbol required
  - THC content per unit and per package
  - Licensed manufacturer + OMMA license number
  - Batch/lot number linked to Metrc
