## Part 1: Missouri Cannabis Regulatory Timeline

### 2018: Proposition 2 — Medical Program Launch
Missouri voters approved Amendment 2 (Prop 2) in November 2018. Medical marijuana program launched in 2019. DHSS/DCR built the regulatory infrastructure from scratch under tight timelines.

### 2019–2022: Medical-Only Program
Missouri operated a medical-only program through 2022. DCR's inspection and enforcement infrastructure was calibrated for a medical market.

### November 2022: Amendment 3 — Adult-Use Approval
Voters approved Amendment 3 (adult-use). The amendment required DCR to license adult-use operators and begin sales within 6 months. The rules for adult-use operations — 19 CSR 100 updates — were published on a compressed timeline.

**Key changes from Amendment 3:**
- Dual-track (medical + adult-use) dispensary operations authorized
- Microbusiness license class created with social equity priority
- Home cultivation (6 plants) authorized
- Transporter license requirement extended to adult-use
- 90-day retention standard applied across all license types
- Social equity expungement provisions activated

### February 3, 2023: Adult-Use Sales Launch
Missouri began adult-use retail sales. Medical marijuana operators converted to dual-track operations. New adult-use applicants began receiving licenses.

### 2023–2025: DCR Maturation Phase
DCR scaled its inspection capacity, issued its first adult-use corrective actions, and began addressing the compliance gaps from the speed launch. Enforcement has increased year-over-year.
