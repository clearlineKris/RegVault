## Executive Summary

Missouri's adult-use market is three years operational. The DCR has moved from a launch-tolerant enforcement posture to active compliance enforcement. The 2026 risk profile reflects the accumulated findings from the first three years of enforcement and the emerging priorities of a maturing agency.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 1: Dual-Track Dispensary Compliance

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High  
**Trigger:** 19 CSR 100-2.010(9); DCR enforcement 2023–2025

Missouri's dual-track dispensaries — serving both medical patients (verification required) and adult-use customers (no verification) — face a daily operational compliance challenge that DCR is actively inspecting. Patient verification failures on medical transactions are the top dispensary compliance finding category.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Are medical and adult-use transaction workflows operationally distinct at every POS station?
- Are staff trained specifically on when patient verification is required vs. not required?
- Have the last 30 days of medical transaction records been reviewed against patient registry entries?

**Recommended Posture:** Physical or procedural transaction track separation. POS systems that flag medical vs. adult-use at the transaction level are the most defensible.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 2: 90-Day Camera Retention — Infrastructure

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Infrastructure  
**Trigger:** 19 CSR 100-3.010(2)

Missouri's 90-day retention requirement is one of the toughest in the country. DCR inspectors are testing retention depth as a standard inspection step. Medical-era security systems not upgraded for adult-use launch are the primary source of retention non-compliance.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Have all facilities' DVR/NVR systems been audited for 90-day retention capacity?
- Have you tested footage retrieval from Day 85 at each facility?
- Were medical-program security systems updated before adult-use launch?

**Recommended Posture:** Run the storage test at every facility. Pull Day 85 footage. If it's unavailable, the fix is storage, not policy.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 3: Pesticide Residue — Testing Network Maturation Effect

**Risk Level:** 🟠 High  
**Trigger:** 19 CSR 100-4.010; DCR enforcement 2024–2026

As Missouri's testing laboratory network has matured, pesticide residue findings have increased. The supply-chain contamination problem is real — clones and biomass entering the supply chain from sources without pesticide disclosure are a primary vector.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Is pre-harvest pesticide residue testing in your current protocol?
- Are all pesticide applicators (in-house + contractors) verified for current MDA credentials?
- Are pesticide use disclosures required from all clone and biomass suppliers?

**Recommended Posture:** Pre-harvest testing is the only early warning. One positive test result post-harvest triggers a product quarantine and potential recall — pre-harvest testing catches it before it reaches that point.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 4: Microbusiness Compliance Support

**Risk Level:** 🟡 Medium  
**Trigger:** DCR compliance outreach pattern 2024–2025

DCR's targeted compliance outreach to Microbusiness operators signals this segment has above-average compliance gaps. If you hold a Microbusiness license or acquired one, it warrants specific compliance attention.

**Recommended Posture:** Dedicated compliance review for Microbusiness operations. If you don't have compliance staff capacity, a shared compliance service provider is a reasonable solution.
