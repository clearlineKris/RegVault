## Missouri Regulatory Framework Overview

**Governing Authority:** Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services (DHSS), Division of Cannabis Regulation (DCR).

Missouri operates a dual-track system: medical marijuana (Amendment 2/Prop 2, 2018) and adult-use (Amendment 3, Nov. 2022; retail sales launched February 3, 2023). Both tracks operate under DCR jurisdiction.

Missouri's cannabis program is notable for strong social equity provisions in Amendment 3 — including equity licensing, expungement requirements, and microbusiness set-asides.

## Section 1: Licensing Obligations

### 1.1 Statutory Foundation
- **Mo. Rev. Stat. § 195.17**: Core cannabis regulatory authority
- **19 CSR 100-1.010 et seq.**: DCR operational rules for all license types

### 1.2 License Categories — Adult-Use (2026 Operative)

| License Type | Reg. Ref | Key Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive Cultivator | 19 CSR 100-1.030 | Various facility tiers; canopy limits by tier |
| Comprehensive Processor/Manufacturer | 19 CSR 100-1.040 | Extraction method disclosure to DCR |
| Comprehensive Dispensary | 19 CSR 100-1.050 | Adult-use + medical sales; patient verification for medical |
| Microbusiness | 19 CSR 100-1.060 | Vertically integrated; social equity priority; smaller scale |
| Testing Laboratory | 19 CSR 100-1.070 | ISO 17025 accreditation; independence required |
| Transporter | 19 CSR 100-1.080 | Required for all inter-facility product movement |
| Wholesale | 19 CSR 100-1.090 | Product sourcing and distribution |

### 1.3 Microbusiness Set-Aside — Social Equity
Amendment 3 created a specific Microbusiness license class with social equity priority. Microbusiness licenses are set-aside for qualifying applicants including those with prior cannabis convictions, residents of disproportionately impacted areas, and veterans. This track has a separate application and scoring process.

### 1.4 Compliance Checklist — Licensing

- [ ] Current DCR license certificate posted at licensed premises (19 CSR 100-1.010(3))
- [ ] Ownership, beneficial interest, and management disclosures current (19 CSR 100-1.010(5))
- [ ] Background checks current for all required personnel
- [ ] Annual renewal filed within DCR renewal window
- [ ] Local authorization/zoning current alongside state license
- [ ] Microbusiness operators: social equity eligibility documentation maintained if applicable

## Section 2: Seed-to-Sale Tracking

### 2.1 Statutory Foundation
- **Mo. Rev. Stat. § 195.17(4)**: Mandatory seed-to-sale tracking
- **19 CSR 100-2.010 et seq.**: Metrc tracking requirements
- Missouri uses **Metrc** as its statewide tracking system

### 2.2 Metrc Obligations — 2026

| Obligation | Rule Ref | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Plant tag at propagation | 19 CSR 100-2.010(1) | Day of propagation |
| Harvest batch creation | 19 CSR 100-2.010(3) | Day of harvest |
| Transfer manifest (pre-departure) | 19 CSR 100-2.020 | Before vehicle moves |
| Inventory reconciliation | 19 CSR 100-2.010(7) | Weekly; documented |
| Dual-track reconciliation (medical/adult-use) | 19 CSR 100-2.010(9) | Daily for dual-track operators |
| Discrepancy report | 19 CSR 100-2.010(8) | Within 3 business days |

### 2.3 Compliance Checklist — Tracking

- [ ] All plants tagged at propagation (19 CSR 100-2.010(1))
- [ ] Harvest batches created same-day (19 CSR 100-2.010(3))
- [ ] Transfer manifests before departure (19 CSR 100-2.020)
- [ ] Weekly Metrc reconciliation against physical count (19 CSR 100-2.010(7))
- [ ] Dual-track (medical/adult-use) Metrc segregated (19 CSR 100-2.010(9))
- [ ] Discrepancy reports within 3 business days (19 CSR 100-2.010(8))

## Section 3: Security & Access Control

### 3.1 Rule Foundation
- **19 CSR 100-3.010**: Security requirements
  - Camera coverage: all entry/exit, all storage, all sales, all cultivation, all processing
  - Retention: **90-day** minimum
  - Visitor log and restricted access
- **19 CSR 100-3.020**: Alarm systems — 24/7 monitoring, panic buttons

### 3.2 Security Requirements Matrix

| Area | Camera Required | Retention | Restricted |
|---|---|---|---|
| All exterior entry/exit | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Dispensary/retail floor | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Vault/storage | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Processing/manufacturing | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Cultivation canopy | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Loading/unloading | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |

### 3.3 Compliance Checklist — Security

- [ ] 90-day footage retention storage capacity verified (19 CSR 100-3.010(2))
- [ ] Camera coverage audit against current facility footprint
- [ ] Visitor log: name, date, time, purpose, escort employee
- [ ] 24/7 alarm monitoring contract current (19 CSR 100-3.020)
- [ ] Security plan updated within 30 days of material change

## Section 4: Pesticide Compliance

### 4.1 Rule Foundation
- **19 CSR 100-4.010**: Pesticide and chemical input requirements
  - EPA-registered pesticides only
  - Missouri Department of Agriculture (MDA) pesticide applicator credential for commercial applications
  - Application log required
  - 48-hour re-entry interval
  - IPM plan required

---

## Section 5: Packaging & Labeling

### 5.1 Rule Foundation
- **19 CSR 100-5.010**: Product packaging and labeling standards
  - Child-resistant packaging
  - Missouri universal symbol required
  - THC content per serving and per package
  - Licensed manufacturer + DCR license number
  - Batch/lot number linked to Metrc
