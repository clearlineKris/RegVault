## Missouri Pesticide Compliance Framework

**Two-authority structure:**
1. **DCR (19 CSR 100-4.010)**: Cannabis-specific pesticide rules — permitted products, application log, re-entry intervals, IPM plan
2. **MDA (RSMo § 281.005 et seq.)**: Missouri Pesticide Use Act — commercial applicator licensing for business operations

## Testing Network Maturation Effect

Missouri's testing laboratory network was thin at adult-use launch. By 2025–2026, the network has expanded significantly. The implication: pesticide compounds that were not detected in the under-resourced 2023 testing environment are being detected now. This is not a new contamination problem — it's an old contamination problem becoming visible.

**The supply-chain vector:** Clone suppliers and biomass sources that operated without pesticide discipline in the medical era are the primary source of cross-contamination into the adult-use supply chain. Requiring pesticide use disclosure from suppliers is the first line of defense.

---

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — Missouri

- [ ] All pesticides on premises EPA-registered and on DCR permitted list (19 CSR 100-4.010)
- [ ] Application log current and complete with MDA license numbers
- [ ] All commercial applicators hold current MDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator license
- [ ] IPM contractors verified for MDA Pesticide Business License + applicator credentials
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented and enforced
- [ ] IPM plan on file with DCR, current version
- [ ] Supplier pesticide disclosure obtained for all clone/plant/biomass purchases
- [ ] Pre-harvest pesticide residue testing in protocol
