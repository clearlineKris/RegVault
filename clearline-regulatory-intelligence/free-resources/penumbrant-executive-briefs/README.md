## Current Papers

### METRC Series

This collection focuses on ambiguities surrounding METRC (Marijuana Enforcement Tracking Reporting Compliance), the seed-to-sale tracking system used by multiple states.

1. **[METRC and Multi-State Compliance Ambiguities](METRC-Multi-State-Compliance-Ambiguities.md)**
   - Explores the perceived ambiguity surrounding METRC as a SaaS solution across states
   - Examines interoperability gaps between METRC and non-METRC states
   - Analyzes multi-jurisdictional tensions and enforcement inconsistencies

2. **[The METRC Data Sovereignty Paradox](METRC-Data-Sovereignty-Paradox.md)**
   - Investigates who owns compliance data: operators, states, or METRC (Franwell)
   - Addresses privacy concerns and federal access implications
   - Explores the tension between mandatory participation and data rights

3. **[Shadow Inventory: METRC Implementation Gaps Across States](METRC-Shadow-Inventory-Implementation-Gaps.md)**
   - Documents regulatory blind spots in seed-to-sale tracking
   - Examines homegrow exemptions, microbusiness gaps, and waste handling
   - Analyzes implications for market transparency and enforcement

## Document Structure

Each Penumbrant Paper follows a consistent structure:

- **Introduction**: Context, scope, and central questions
- **Main Sections**: Detailed analysis organized by theme
  - a. Statutory and Regulatory Ambiguities
  - b. Enforcement Discretion
  - c. Multi-Jurisdictional Tensions
  - d. Case Studies and Real-World Implications
  - e. Stakeholder Asymmetry Analysis
- **Conclusion**: Summary, implications (on operations and posture) & navigation guidance

## Key Focus Areas

As outlined in the [updated guidance](../pull_request_comments/1.md), Penumbrant Papers address:

1. **Statutory and Regulatory Ambiguities** - Vague language, undefined terms, and gaps in coverage
2. **Enforcement Discretion** - Inconsistent application across jurisdictions or agencies
3. **Multi-Jurisdictional Tensions** - Conflicts between overlapping regulatory authorities
4. **Case Law and Precedents** - How courts and agencies are filling regulatory gaps
5. **Stakeholder Asymmetry Analysis** - How information and power imbalances are exacerbated by regulatory ambiguity
