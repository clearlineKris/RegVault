## New York Regulatory Framework Overview

**Governing Authorities:**
- **Office of Cannabis Management (OCM)**: Primary licensing, inspection, and enforcement authority for adult-use, medical, and hemp
- **Cannabis Control Board (CCB)**: Rulemaking and licensing policy authority; 5-member board
- **MRTA**: Marihuana Regulation and Taxation Act — signed March 31, 2021; the foundational adult-use statute
- **New York Cannabis Law**: Codifies the full cannabis program including medical (formerly DOH-regulated), adult-use, cannabinoid hemp, and microbusiness

New York operates a consolidated framework: the OCM governs medical, adult-use, and hemp under a single agency. The medical program transitioned from DOH to OCM in 2022.

## Section 1: Licensing Obligations

### 1.1 Statutory Foundation
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 61**: Adult-use cultivator license
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 62**: Adult-use processor license
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 63**: Adult-use retail dispensary license
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 67**: Microbusiness license
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 68**: Delivery license
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 76**: Registered Organization (legacy medical)
- **9 NYCRR Part 116**: OCM operational rules

### 1.2 License Categories — Adult-Use (2026)

| License Type | Statute | Key Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Adult-Use Cultivator | § 61 | Canopy limits by tier; outdoor and indoor |
| Adult-Use Processor | § 62 | Extraction method disclosure |
| Conditional Adult-Use Retail Dispensary (CAURD) | § 63 | Social equity — prior cannabis conviction + existing business |
| Adult-Use Retail Dispensary (AURD) | § 63 | Standard retail; applications opened 2024 |
| Microbusiness | § 67 | Vertically integrated; ≤10,000 sq ft; self-supply |
| Delivery | § 68 | Standalone delivery service |
| Registered Organization (Registered Organization / RO) | § 76 | Legacy medical operators — converted to adult-use vertical license |
| On-Site Consumption | § 63 | Pending final rules as of 2026 |

### 1.3 CAURD Program — The Social Equity Priority Track
The Conditional Adult-Use Retail Dispensary (CAURD) program was New York's first adult-use retail licensing pathway, prioritizing applicants who had a prior cannabis conviction (or whose family member did) AND owned an existing lawful business in New York. CAURD licenses were the first adult-use retail licenses issued in New York (beginning late 2022/2023).

**CAURD legal challenges:** The CAURD program faced multiple legal challenges in 2023–2024 based on claims of unfair competitive advantage. Courts issued and lifted injunctions during this period. The legal landscape for CAURD operators in 2026 reflects a program that survived but was significantly delayed by litigation.

### 1.4 Compliance Checklist — Licensing

- [ ] Current OCM license certificate posted at licensed premises (9 NYCRR § 116.1(e))
- [ ] Ownership, beneficial interest, and management disclosures current (N.Y. Cannabis Law § 43)
- [ ] Background checks current for all required principal officers and owners
- [ ] Annual renewal filed within CCB/OCM renewal window
- [ ] CAURD operators: social equity eligibility documentation maintained
- [ ] Local municipality: no opt-out (N.Y. Cannabis Law § 131 — municipalities may opt out of retail dispensaries)

## Section 2: Seed-to-Sale Tracking (Metrc)

### 2.1 Statutory Foundation
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 51**: Mandatory seed-to-sale tracking
- **9 NYCRR Part 116**: OCM Metrc tracking requirements
- New York uses **Metrc** as its statewide tracking system (implemented 2023)

### 2.2 Metrc Obligations — 2026

| Obligation | Rule Ref | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Plant tag at propagation | 9 NYCRR § 116.5(a) | Day of propagation |
| Harvest batch creation | 9 NYCRR § 116.5(b) | Day of harvest |
| Transfer manifest (pre-departure) | 9 NYCRR § 116.6 | Before vehicle moves |
| Inventory reconciliation | 9 NYCRR § 116.5(e) | Weekly; documented |
| Discrepancy report | 9 NYCRR § 116.5(f) | Within 3 business days |

**New York-specific Metrc note:** Metrc launched in New York in 2023 alongside adult-use. Some legacy Registered Organization (RO) operators had to migrate from prior tracking systems. Migration-era data quality issues may persist in 2026 inventory records.

### 2.3 Compliance Checklist — Tracking

- [ ] All plants tagged in Metrc at propagation (9 NYCRR § 116.5(a))
- [ ] Harvest batches created same-day (9 NYCRR § 116.5(b))
- [ ] Transfer manifests generated pre-departure (9 NYCRR § 116.6)
- [ ] Weekly inventory reconciliation against Metrc (9 NYCRR § 116.5(e))
- [ ] Discrepancy reports within 3 business days (9 NYCRR § 116.5(f))
- [ ] Legacy RO operators: prior-system migration data audited for Metrc accuracy

## Section 3: Security & Access Control

### 3.1 Rule Foundation
- **9 NYCRR § 116.8**: Security requirements
  - Camera coverage: all entry/exit, all storage, all sales, all cultivation, all processing
  - Retention: **90-day** minimum
  - Visitor log: name, date, time, purpose, escort
  - Security plan on file with OCM

### 3.2 Security Requirements Matrix

| Area | Camera Required | Retention | Restricted |
|---|---|---|---|
| All exterior entry/exit | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Retail/dispensary floor | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Vault/storage | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Processing/extraction | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Cultivation canopy | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |
| Loading/receiving | ✅ | 90 days | ✅ |

## Section 4: Pesticide Compliance

### 4.1 Rule Foundation
- **9 NYCRR § 116.10**: Pesticide and chemical input requirements
  - EPA-registered pesticides only
  - New York State Department of Environmental Conservation (NYSDEC) pesticide applicator certification required for commercial applications
  - Application log required
  - 48-hour re-entry interval
  - IPM plan on file with OCM

**New York-specific:** NYSDEC is the pesticide authority in New York. Commercial pesticide applications at cannabis facilities must be made by or under supervision of a NYSDEC-certified commercial pesticide applicator.

## Section 5: Packaging & Labeling

### 5.1 Rule Foundation
- **N.Y. Cannabis Law § 56**: Packaging and labeling requirements
- **9 NYCRR § 116.12**: OCM product labeling standards
  - Child-resistant packaging
  - New York universal cannabis symbol required
  - THC content per serving and per package
  - "Contains Cannabis — Keep Away from Children" statement
  - Licensed manufacturer + OCM license number
  - Metrc batch/lot number

## Section 7: Municipal Opt-Out — New York Framework

**N.Y. Cannabis Law § 131**: Municipalities (cities, towns, villages) could opt out of permitting adult-use retail dispensaries and on-site consumption sites by December 31, 2021 (initial deadline). Over 1,000 municipalities opted out. Subsequent legislation has modified opt-out and opt-in procedures.

**2026 status:** Any dispensary operating in a municipality that opted out faces license invalidity risk. Verify current opt-out/opt-in status for every operating location before any new application or expansion.
