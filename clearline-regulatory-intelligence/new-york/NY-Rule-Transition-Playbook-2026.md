> *"New York's cannabis history is compressed into five years: MRTA in 2021, CAURD in 2022, injunctions in 2023, stabilization in 2024–2025. Every transition created compliance obligations that operators had to absorb at speed. This playbook documents what happened and what's still open."*

---

## Part 1: New York Cannabis Regulatory Timeline

### March 2021: MRTA Signed
The Marihuana Regulation and Taxation Act was signed by Governor Cuomo. The most comprehensive cannabis legalization bill in U.S. history at the time — consolidated medical and adult-use under OCM, created the CCB, and established the CAURD program concept.

### 2021: Municipal Opt-Out Window
Municipalities had until December 31, 2021 to opt out of adult-use retail dispensaries and on-site consumption. Over 1,000 municipalities opted out. The opt-out map shaped the viability of retail locations across the state.

### 2022: OCM Established; CAURD Applications Open
The Office of Cannabis Management became operational. CAURD applications opened in late 2022 — the first adult-use licensing pathway, prioritizing applicants with prior cannabis convictions and existing business experience.

### Late 2022 / 2023: First CAURD Retail Openings; Legal Challenges Begin
First CAURD dispensaries opened in late 2022. Legal challenges from excluded applicants followed. Courts issued injunctions in 2023 that delayed additional CAURD licensing.

### 2023: Metrc Launch; Medical Program Transfer Complete
Metrc launched in New York. The medical marijuana program (formerly under DOH) completed its transfer to OCM. Legacy Registered Organizations began operating under OCM oversight.

**Compliance impact:** Metrc migration created data quality issues for legacy RO operators. New cultivators/processors onboarded to Metrc alongside CAURD retailers — creating a high-volume simultaneous launch.

### 2024–2025: CAURD Litigation Resolution; AURD Applications
CAURD litigation largely resolved by 2024. Standard Adult-Use Retail Dispensary (AURD) applications opened. The broader licensee base began to expand beyond CAURD.

### 2025–2026: OCM Enforcement Maturation
OCM enforcement has intensified with a growing inspection team. Priority focus areas: Metrc compliance, security (90-day retention), and unlicensed market competition enforcement.
