## New York Pesticide Compliance Framework

**Two-authority structure:**
1. **OCM (9 NYCRR § 116.10)**: Cannabis-specific pesticide rules — permitted products, application log, re-entry intervals, IPM plan
2. **NYSDEC (ECL § 33-0101 et seq.)**: New York's pesticide law — commercial pesticide applicator certification for business operations; applies to cannabis cultivation facilities

---

## NYSDEC Applicator Certification Requirement

New York's Environmental Conservation Law requires commercial pesticide applications at a business operation to be made by or under the supervision of a NYSDEC-certified commercial pesticide applicator. Cannabis Grower/Processors are covered.

**Verification requirements:**
- In-house cultivation staff applying pesticides: NYSDEC Commercial Pesticide Applicator certification
- IPM contractors: NYSDEC Business License (Pesticide Registration) + applicator certification
- Annual renewal; verify status before every application

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — New York

- [ ] All pesticides on premises EPA-registered and on OCM permitted list (9 NYCRR § 116.10)
- [ ] Application log current and complete with NYSDEC certification numbers
- [ ] All commercial applicators hold current NYSDEC certification
- [ ] IPM contractors verified for NYSDEC Business License (Pesticide Registration) + applicator cert
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented and enforced
- [ ] IPM plan on file with OCM, current version
- [ ] Supplier pesticide disclosure obtained for all plant/clone/biomass purchases
