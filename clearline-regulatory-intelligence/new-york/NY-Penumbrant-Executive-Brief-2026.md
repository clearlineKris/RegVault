## Executive Summary

New York's adult-use market has survived a turbulent first three years — legal challenges, licensing delays, unlicensed market competition, and OCM resource constraints. The 2026 environment reflects a market that is stabilizing: CAURD litigation is largely resolved, standard AURD licenses are issuing, and OCM enforcement is intensifying. The compliance risk profile is concentrated around Metrc integrity, municipal opt-in currency, security infrastructure gaps, and the lingering impacts of the unlicensed market competition.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 1: Metrc Data Integrity — Legacy and New Operators

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High  
**Trigger:** 9 NYCRR § 116.5; OCM inspection pattern 2024–2026

New York's Metrc implementation had structural challenges. Legacy RO operators have migration-era data quality issues; new operators are still learning the system. OCM is finding Metrc discrepancies in both categories.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Have legacy RO migration-era Metrc records been audited and reconciled?
- Are your current Metrc inventory records clean and reconcilable against physical inventory?
- Are Metrc discrepancy reports being filed within 3 business days of discovery?

**Recommended Posture:** Commission a Metrc data quality audit — internal or third-party — that specifically reviews migration-era records (for ROs) and identifies any structural discrepancy patterns in current records.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 2: Municipal Opt-In/Opt-Out Currency

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Structural  
**Trigger:** N.Y. Cannabis Law § 131; OCM database

The municipal opt-in map changes. Any licensing action — new location, expansion — requires verification of current opt-in status through the OCM's official database at the time of application.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Has the opt-in status of every municipality where you operate or are considering operating been verified against the current OCM database?
- Is municipal opt-in status verification included in your licensing expansion checklist?

**Recommended Posture:** Treat municipal opt-in status as a "verify before every action" item, not a "verified once" item.

## Priority Risk Domain 3: 90-Day Security Retention

**Risk Level:** 🟠 High — Infrastructure  
**Trigger:** 9 NYCRR § 116.8

Same infrastructure gap as Ohio, Michigan, Missouri. OCM is testing it.

**Recommended Posture:** Storage capacity audit + Day 85 footage test. Especially critical for high-camera-count retail dispensaries where storage requirements are highest.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 4: Unlicensed Market Competition — Compliance Context

**Risk Level:** 🟡 Strategic  
**Trigger:** Market environment

The unlicensed market is not a direct compliance violation risk for licensed operators — but it shapes the compliance environment. OCM enforcement intensity is being driven by licensed operator advocacy. The more aggressively licensed operators engage with OCM on unlicensed competition, the more OCM enforcement resources flow to the unlicensed market — freeing licensed operators from the compliance cost disadvantage.

**Recommended Posture:** Active engagement with OCM and industry associations on unlicensed market enforcement. Document unlicensed competitors in your market. This is compliance-adjacent: a clean licensed operation that reports unlicensed competitors supports enforcement outcomes that level the playing field.
