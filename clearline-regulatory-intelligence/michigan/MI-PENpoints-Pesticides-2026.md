## Michigan Pesticide Compliance Framework

**Two-authority structure:**
1. **CRA (R 420.402)**: Cannabis-specific pesticide rules — permitted products, application log, re-entry intervals, IPM plan
2. **MDARD (Michigan Pesticide Control Act, MCL 324.8301 et seq.)**: Commercial pesticide applicator licensing — applies to cannabis facilities as agricultural operations

---

## MDARD Applicator License Requirement

Michigan's Pesticide Control Act requires that commercial pesticide applications be made by or under the direct supervision of an MDARD-licensed commercial pesticide applicator. This applies to cannabis cultivation facilities.

**What to verify:**
- In-house IPM staff must hold MDARD Commercial Pesticide Applicator license (or operate under one)
- IPM contractors must hold MDARD Pesticide Business License + appropriate applicator credentials
- Annual license renewal — license lapses create an immediate compliance gap

## Michigan-Specific: Price Crash Supply Chain Risk

Michigan's wholesale price collapse created a secondary pesticide risk: distressed cultivators sometimes sold trim and biomass to processors without adequate pesticide disclosure. Processors extracting from contaminated biomass face test failures for compounds they didn't apply.

**Mitigation:** Require pesticide use declarations from all biomass/trim suppliers. Include batch-specific pesticide history as a purchase condition.

---

## Compliance Hardening Checklist — Michigan

- [ ] All pesticides on premises EPA-registered and on CRA permitted list (R 420.402(1))
- [ ] Application log current and complete with MDARD license numbers
- [ ] All commercial applicators hold current MDARD license
- [ ] IPM contractors verified for MDARD Pesticide Business License + applicator credentials
- [ ] 48-hour re-entry intervals documented and enforced (R 420.402(4))
- [ ] IPM plan filed with CRA (R 420.402(6)) and current
- [ ] Supplier pesticide disclosure obtained for all trim/biomass purchases
- [ ] Pre-harvest pesticide residue testing in protocol
