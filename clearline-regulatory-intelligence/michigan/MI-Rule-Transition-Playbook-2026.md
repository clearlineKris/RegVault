## Part 1: Key Regulatory Transitions — 2019 to Present

### Transition 1: MRTMA Adult-Use Launch — December 2019
Adult-use sales began December 1, 2019. Rules under R 420 applied to all adult-use licensees from day one. Many operators launched while still holding provisional licenses pending full CRA approval.

### Transition 2: CRA Administrative Rules Update — 2022
CRA updated R 420 rules in 2022 to address enforcement gaps identified in years one and two, including:
- Clarified Class C stacking "separate operations" language (though ambiguity remains)
- Tightened Metrc discrepancy reporting from 48 hours to 24 hours
- Added security plan update requirement within 14 days of material changes

**Operator impact:** Operators who had operational practices calibrated to pre-2022 rules and did not update their SOPs after the amendment are the primary corrective action targets in 2024–2026.

### Transition 3: Provisional-to-Full License Conversion — 2022–2025
CRA systematically converted provisional licenses to full licenses. Full license obligations apply fully from conversion date. Pre-conversion practices not grandfathered.

### Transition 4: Consolidation-Driven Acquisition Compliance Issues — 2023–2025
Market consolidation led to acquisitions of distressed operators. Acquiring entities became responsible for the compliance history of acquired licenses, including pending corrective action plans.
