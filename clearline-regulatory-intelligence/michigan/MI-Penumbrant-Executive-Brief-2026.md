## Executive Summary

Michigan's adult-use market has matured through a rapid growth phase, a price collapse, and a consolidation wave. The Cannabis Regulatory Agency (CRA) has moved from a permissive early-market posture to active enforcement of rules that were informally flexible during the boom years. The 2026 compliance environment is defined by the CRA's effort to standardize what "separate operations," "discrepancy reporting," and "municipal authorization" actually mean in practice.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 1: Class C License Stacking — Separation Standard

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High  
**Trigger:** MCL 333.27959(3); CRA enforcement 2024–2025

The separate operations requirement for Class C stacking has moved from a theoretical compliance question to an active enforcement category. Operators with shared infrastructure across stacked licenses are receiving corrective actions as CRA's interpretation solidifies.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Do all stacked Class C licenses have physically designated separate storage and access zones?
- Are Metrc accounts for each license completely segregated with no cross-entries?
- Has a compliance attorney reviewed your stacking structure against CRA's current enforcement posture?

**Recommended Posture:** Physical separation documentation is the minimum defensible standard. If you share any infrastructure, document the separation methodology formally and proactively present it to CRA before inspection.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 2: Metrc 24-Hour Discrepancy Reporting

**Risk Level:** 🔴 High — Operational  
**Trigger:** CRA Administrative Rules R 420.302(8)

Michigan's 24-hour discrepancy reporting window is one of the tightest in the country. Weekend discoveries create operational exposure. Operators who attempt to resolve before reporting compound the violation.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Is there a compliance-on-call protocol to handle Metrc discrepancy discovery outside business hours?
- Do all compliance staff understand that the clock runs from discovery — not from resolution?
- Are discrepancy reports filed as SOP-level required actions, not as discretionary decisions?

**Recommended Posture:** Compliance-on-call coverage for Metrc events is an operational necessity in Michigan, not a luxury. The 24-hour window tolerates no weekend exceptions.

## Priority Risk Domain 3: Municipal Opt-In Monitoring

**Risk Level:** 🟠 High — External  
**Trigger:** MCL 333.27956; local policy volatility

Municipal authorization is an ongoing obligation, not a one-time acquisition. Municipalities that have opted in can impose new conditions, restrict operations, or reverse opt-in status. Operators who treat municipal authorization as a "check the box and move on" item are exposed.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Is municipal authorization formally documented and stored in your compliance file?
- Is someone monitoring local council agendas in your operating municipality(ies)?
- Do you have a relationship with your municipality that would alert you to pending policy changes before they become effective?

**Recommended Posture:** Assign someone to monitor local council agendas in every operating municipality — monthly at minimum. Build a local government relations function, even informally.

---

## Priority Risk Domain 4: 90-Day Camera Retention — Infrastructure

**Risk Level:** 🟠 High — Infrastructure  
**Trigger:** CRA Administrative Rules R 420.502(2)

Michigan's 90-day retention requirement matches Ohio's and exceeds most states. Same storage capacity calculation problem applies.

**Executive Decision Points:**
- Has your security vendor confirmed 90-day retention at your camera count and resolution?
- Have you tested footage retrieval from Day 88?

---

## Priority Risk Domain 5: Provisional-to-Full License Compliance Gap

**Risk Level:** 🟡 Medium — Transitional  
**Trigger:** CRA provisional license transition 2023–2025

Operators who transitioned from provisional to full license in 2023–2025 may have compliance gaps between provisional-tolerated practices and full-license requirements.

**Recommended Posture:** If you transitioned in 2023–2025, conduct a full compliance gap audit against the current R 420 rule set.
