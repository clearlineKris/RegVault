### Teaser

Illinois launched adult-use cannabis with the nation's most ambitious social equity program, but **creating opportunity while maintaining market stability has produced regulatory gray areas that challenge operators, applicants, and regulators alike.**

From point-based application scoring to conditional licenses stuck in limbo, Illinois is attempting to correct decades of drug war disparities through cannabis licensing. This Margin Notes document investigates:

- **Social Equity Verification**: Who qualifies as a social equity applicant, and what documentation proves it? The definition is clear until it isn't
- **Conditional License Challenges**: Thousands of conditional licenses were awarded, but zoning, financing, and real estate requirements create impossible hurdles for many recipients
- **Craft Grow Limitations**: Small cultivator licenses were designed to promote equity, but supply agreements and wholesale market access create operational puzzles
- **Infuser vs. Processor Distinctions**: When does food manufacturing cross into cannabis processing, and which regulations apply to hybrid operations?
- **Multi-License Ownership Caps**: The points system limits license concentration, but what constitutes "true party of interest" when investors use complex structures?

**Illinois's bold experiment**: Proof that progressive policy goals create as many regulatory questions as they answer.
